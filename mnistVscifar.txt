MNIST and CIFAR-10 are image classification datasets.. Cifar-10 is way harder.

MNIST has 28×28 grayscale images of digits. They are simple and clean.

The digits range from 0 to 9.

CIFAR-10 has 32×32 color images. They are from 10 real-world object categories.

These include airplanes, cats, dogs and trucks.

These images have backgrounds.

The lighting conditions vary.

The objects are in positions.

There is a lot of diversity.

CIFAR-10 images have three color channels. RGB.

This means the model has to process information.

To get accuracy on CIFAR-10 you usually need deeper CNN architectures.

You also need data augmentation and longer training.

This is different from MNIST.

MNIST is simpler.

CIFAR-10 requires more.

It needs data, more processing power and more complex models.

The model has to be able to handle backgrounds and varying lighting conditions.

It has to be able to recognize objects, in positions.

That is what makes CIFAR-10 challenging.
